About Me

“Have the courage to follow your heart and intuition. They somehow already know what you truly want to become. Everything else is secondary.” - Steve Jobs

Hi there! My name is Manu Karthik Maddali. Welcome to my website!

4 years ago I was graduating high school, keen on becoming a gastroenterologist. So much, that my senior project was based on it!

But that summer before college changed everything. I wanted to see what my backup plan could be in case my current one didn’t pan out, so I decided to look into Computer Science. I was always interested in CS but never thought that I would get so deep into it. Boy did I! By the end of summer, I was so dedicated that I decided to pursue CS wholeheartedly. Even though it was my passion, I never found it comforting to learn it in class. Thus, I began my journey of self-teaching.

The first language I taught myself was Python, and I fell in love with it. So versatile and flexible, yet I didn't know how to put it to good use. That is, until, I came across Data Science. I knew I had the tools to excel in it and took up many online courses to master it. Which I accomplished in a year. But that still wasn't enough. I was hungry for more. Independent side projects weren’t satisfying my need to learn anymore. I NEEDED real-world experience where I solved REAL problems. So, I decided to get an internship in Data Science.

120+ internships and 4 interviews later, I landed a job as a Data Analyst Intern for a supply chain company (I was the only Data Analyst for the entire company, crazy right?). At my internship, I found a problem that my department faced, and I realized I could solve it using Python. After 2 months I came out with an MVP for that product and implemented it to use for our department. As August neared, I knew my internship was coming to an end, but to my surprise, the CFO decided to extend it till October! I worked there until October and bid farewell to the company that taught me so much and allowed me to grow and excel in every way possible.

Then came 2020... After our classes went online, I headed back home to my parents and realized I had so much free time. I wanted to learn a new skill. App Development caught my eye. I learned Swift and Xcode in a span of two months and built a few simple apps to showcase it.

I hope this little life story gave you a good idea of what my skills and mindset consist of.

Want to know more? Let's get on a Zoom call, or better yet, grab a cup of coffee!
